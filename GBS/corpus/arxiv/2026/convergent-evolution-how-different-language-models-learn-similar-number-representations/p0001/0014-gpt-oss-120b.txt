The Fourier power ΦT only guarantees a large total variance among the class means, which drives
ΦT =Tr(S B)/N in the numerator. It ensures the class centers are dispersed in space. However,
actual linear separability depends on how this signal aligns with the within-class scatter,SW. A large
condition number, cond(SW ) =λ max(SW )/λmin(SW ), drastically lowers the minimum bound. If
the periodic signal aligns with the dimensions of maximum within-class noise, the massive ΦT is
entirely swallowed by within-class variance. This drives λmax toward zero and forces the entire
(T−1)-dimensional subspace to collapse into overlapping classes.
