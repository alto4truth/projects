6.1 The Mechanics of the Representation
Bottleneck
To understand why scaling fails, we must examine the informationtheoretic capacity mismatch inherent in the Vision Encoder-ProjectorLLM paradigm. Visual data manifolds (e.g., the topology of a molecular graph or the high-frequency pixel variations in a medical scan)
are continuous, high-dimensional, and dense. Conversely, textual
token spaces are discrete, sequential, and highly compressed.

to pass through a narrow, fixed-capacity cross-attention or projection head to be translated into text-like embeddings. As the LLM
