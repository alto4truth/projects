Abstract
The rapid proliferation of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) is widely
celebrated as the dawn of unified multimodal knowledge discovery
but its foundation operates on a dangerous, unquestioned axiom:
that current VLMs faithfully synthesise multimodal data. We argue
they do not. Instead, a profound crisis of trustworthiness underlies
the dominant Vision Encoder-Projector-LLM paradigm. Rather than
extracting grounded knowledge from visual inputs, state-of-theart models frequently exhibit functional blindness, i.e., exploiting
strong language priors to bypass severe visual representation bottlenecks. In this work, we challenge the conventional methodology of
multimodal evaluation, which relies on data ablation or new dataset
creation and therefore fatally conflates dataset biases with architectural incapacity. We propose a radical, information-theoretic
