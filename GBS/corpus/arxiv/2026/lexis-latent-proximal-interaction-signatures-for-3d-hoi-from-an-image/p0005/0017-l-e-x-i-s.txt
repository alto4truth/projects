3.2 LEXIS-Flow
After pretraining a LEXIS codebook of interaction signatures (Sec. 3.1),
we exploit this to reconstruct 3D HOI from an image. To this end, we build
LEXIS-Flow (see Fig. 5 for an overview), a model that captures the conditional
distributionP(B,O | I)via Flow Matching [43]. The generative process is formulated as an Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE) that transports samples
from a Gaussian priorN(0,I)att=0to the target HOI distribution att=1.
Letx= [B;O]denote the HOI state. Then, the flow interpolates between
Gaussian noiseϵ∼ N(0,I)att=0and a ground-truth samplex 1 att=1via
xt = (1−t)ϵ+tx 1. To approximate the vector field that generates this flow, we
train a neural network,vγ(xt, t,I); then the target flow velocity isdxt/dt=x 1−ϵ.
