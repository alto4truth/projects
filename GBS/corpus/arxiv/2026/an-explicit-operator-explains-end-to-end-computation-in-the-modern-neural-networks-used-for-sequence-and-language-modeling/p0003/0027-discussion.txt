The operatorD τ performs a highly structured spectral
factorization of individual input sequences. WhenD τ is
circulant, its eigenvectors are discrete Fourier modes on a
spatially organized ring topology of the network [23, 24],
and different input sequences selectively excite different
subsets of these modes. This selective excitation produces distinct signatures of modes that store information
about the recent past in a manner useful for sequence
classification problems. In the relatively simple sinusoid
dataset, for example, only a small set of modes – those
whose frequencies resonate with inputs in the 15-20 Hz
temporal frequency band – serve to distinguish between
input classes. Although the distribution of energy across
modes is broadly similar across the different input classes,
a specific set of resonant modes exhibit consistent shifts
in amplitude that enable linear separation of the input
classes. This spectral factorization emerges through the
