Main Findings Summary.Our results revealed several key insights about LLM intersectional fairness:
• LLMs perform well in ambiguous contexts, but indeterminate predictions limit subgroup fairness metrics,
while in disambiguated contexts accuracy is influenced by stereotype alignment, with higher performance on
stereotype-reinforcing cases, especially pronounced in race–gender intersections.
• Although subgroup fairness disparities are sometimes low, outcome distributions can still remain uneven across
intersectional groups.
• Across repeated runs, LLMs differ in the consistency of their outputs, showing that model behavior is not only
model-dependent but also sensitive to repeated prompting across contexts and question polarities.
