To achieve this, we leverage two public BBQ-based datasets comprising Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs) designed
for intersectional fairness, introduced in [Parrish et al. 2022], and apply their bias scores while incorporating the more
comprehensive fairness metrics proposed by Meinson et al. [2025], enabling a deeper and more precise assessment
of LLM fairness. We further analyze LLM consistency across repeated runs across contexts, under negative and nonnegative question polarities. This allows us to study not only whether LLMs answer MCQs correctly, but also whether
their wrong answers, subgroup-level output patterns, and repeated responses reveal systematic disparities across
contexts and intersectional groups.
