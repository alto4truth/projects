In addition to the context-specific bias scores sDIS and sAMB introduced by [Parrish et al. 2021], there exist different
intersectional sub-group fairness metrics that were used by [Meinson et al. 2025] to evaluate the intersectional fairness
of different DL models. These metrics are the Statistical Parity (SF) [Kearns et al. 2018] and the Differential Fairness
(DF) [Foulds et al. 2020], measuring the overall disparities and the worst-case gaps between intersectional groups,
respectively.
Not all fairness metrics apply to our dataset, as many are difficult to interpret with a three-class setup including
‘unknown’ option in MCQs with ambiguous contexts or are redundant given bias scores. Under the assumption that
