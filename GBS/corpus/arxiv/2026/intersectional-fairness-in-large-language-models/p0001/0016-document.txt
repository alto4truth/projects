Several BBQ-based studies assess fairness using the bias scores sDIS and sAMB [Chataigner et al. 2025; Liu et al.
2024; Parrish et al. 2021; Yang et al. 2025; Zhuo et al. 2023]. These two metrics are valuable for quantifying specific
stereotype-driven bias in LLMs, given QA-based datasets with both ambiguous and disambiguated contexts. While
the former is designed to calculate the proportion of biased model predictions when the context provides enough
information to answer the question, the latter measures the severity of bias by scaling sDIS with error frequency [Parrish
et al. 2021], reflecting the harm of confidently biased guesses in uncertain scenarios. Additionally, the way bias scores
