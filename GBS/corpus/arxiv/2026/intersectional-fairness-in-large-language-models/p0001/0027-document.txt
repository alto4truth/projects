To the best of our knowledge, the BBQ benchmark dataset [Parrish et al . 2022]—a manually curated benchmark
dataset designed to assess social biases in question-answer systems—is the only benchmark dataset that satisfies all
the aforementioned criteria that make datasets suitable for our LLM models evaluation. BBQ encompasses a range of
identity categories, including age, disability status, gender identity, nationality, physical appearance, race/ethnicity,
religion, Socio-Economic Status (SES), and sexual orientation. It has been used to evaluate both medium-sized LLMs
(e.g., RoBERTa and DeBERTaV3) [Parrish et al. 2022] and large-sized LLMs (e.g., UnifiedQA, InstructGPT, GPT-3, and
ChatGPT) [Li et al . 2023; Parrish et al . 2022] for bias in QA-based datasets. Although BBQ offers a broad scope of
demographic coverage, most of its datasets are not suitable for analyzing intersectional fairness, except for the two
