While some prior studies assessed the fairness of different LLMs on social bias benchmarks such as the Bias
Benchmark Question (BBQ) dataset [Parrish et al. 2021], most focus on a single sensitive attribute at a time, overlooking
intersectional settings, and little attention has been paid to whether LLM outputs are consistent across repeated runs
on the same inputs, a critical property in high stakes decisions where unstable or unpredictable model behavior can be
just as harmful as biased behavior [Jin et al. 2025; Parrish et al. 2021; Saralegi and Zulaika 2025; Simpson et al. 2024].
Therefore, assessing the intersectional fairness and output consistency of LLMs deserves the same level of attention
given to the assessment of their effectiveness and/or efficiency.
