is influenced by stereotype alignment, with models being more accurate when the correct answer reinforces a stereotype than

with stereotype-consistent cues, and no evaluated LLM achieves consistently reliable or fair behavior across intersectional settings.
These findings highlight the need for evaluation beyond accuracy to capture the interplay between bias, subgroup fairness, and
consistency in LLMs, emphasizing the importance of combining these metrics, accounting for intersectional groups, and assessing
model behavior across contexts and repeated runs.
