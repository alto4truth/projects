Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly deployed in socially sensitive settings, where their decisions can impact individuals
and groups, raising concerns about fairness and biases, particularly across intersectional demographic attributes.
In this paper, we systematically evaluate intersectional fairness in six LLM models using both ambiguous and disambiguated
contexts derived from two benchmark datasets. We assess differences in LLM behavior in terms of fairness using bias scores and
subgroup fairness metrics, alongside accuracy. Further, we evaluate LLM consistency through a multi-run analysis across contexts and
across negative and non-negative question polarities.
