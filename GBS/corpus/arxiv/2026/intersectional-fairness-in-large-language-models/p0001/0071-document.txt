Ambiguous Context Most LLMs predominantly select
unknown option in absence of context.
Strong performance in ambiguous contexts alone
should not be treated as sufficient evidence of LLM
fairness.
disambiguated Context Accuracy in disambiguated contexts
is influenced by stereotype alignment.
Models may rely on stereotypes even when sufficient context is provided.
Intersectionality Different patterns across
Race_Gender and Race_SES datasets.
Fairness evaluation must consider intersectional
groups, not single attributes.
Bias Direction (sDIS) Strong tendency toward stereotypealigned outputs (notably in
Race_Gender dataset).
Systems should be tested for directional bias, not
just correctness.
Fairness Metrics (SF/DF) Limited or unstable informativeness
under sparse outcomes
Multiple complementary metrics are needed for robust fairness assessment.
Consistency Response patterns, including stereotyped errors, vary across runs.
Reliability and stability should be part of fairness
evaluation.
