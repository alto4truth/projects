Another limitation lies in how LLM consistency is assessed. More in detail, most prior work does not test repeated
runs of the same prompt but instead relies on indirect checks such as shuffling answer options in MCQ-based datasets,
permuting questions, or even re-prompting with slight variations [Jin et al. 2024; Narnaware et al. 2025; Saffari et al.
2025; Xu et al. 2025; Yang et al. 2025]. The closest setup to assessing consistency across different prompts is [Wang et al.
2025], where the authors use five different random seeds to test controlled, reproducible differences in outputs. This,
however, does not capture the full stochastic variation of LLM behavior.
