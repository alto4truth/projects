Compared with [Parrish et al. 2022], our findings show a different ambiguous-context pattern for modern LLMs:
whereas BBQ reports stronger biased responding under under-informative contexts, our results show that many
models more often select theunknownoption, which makes subgroup fairness metrics less informative in this setting.
In disambiguated contexts, our results diverge from the inconclusive intersectional patterns reported in the BBQ
benchmark. Specifically, we observe consistent and systematic differences across intersectional datasets: Race_Gender
exhibits strong directional bias, while Race_SES reveals bias primarily through performance asymmetries. This suggests
that intersectional bias is not uniformly expressed, but instead depends on how stereotype information interacts with
the prediction task.
