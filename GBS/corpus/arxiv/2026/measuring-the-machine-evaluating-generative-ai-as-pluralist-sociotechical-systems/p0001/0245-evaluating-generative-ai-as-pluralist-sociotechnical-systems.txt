Evaluation as World-Making This thesis began from a simple intuition: evaluation is never neutral. It is world-making. In pluralist and contested spaces, our measures do not merely record; they steer. The Jacquard loom remains in modern AI, but its thread is human values. Our instruments weave patterns we later mistake for the fabric itself. The task is not to find a single canon, but to build measures that reveal rather than prescribe. Such measures must track MaSH Loops: recursive machine-society-human interactions through which meaning, value, and responsibility are enacted rather than read off from the model alone.
What we measure, we amplify. What we amplify becomes.
