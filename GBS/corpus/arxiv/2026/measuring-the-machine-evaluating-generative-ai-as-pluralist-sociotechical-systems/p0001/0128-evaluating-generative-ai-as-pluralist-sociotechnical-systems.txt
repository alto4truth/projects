3.5.2 Market Design by Governments “Market design” refers to deliberate structuring of systems to guide participant behaviour; what some scholars call “smart markets” [130, 198]. Governments often engage in this form of structured intervention. For example, in March 2020 the US government used US treasury auctions to smooth out price swings [330]. Due to the impact on society of this method of market manipulation, market design has been seen by some scholars as a form of social engineering [330]. Singapore’s Government Land Sales (GLS) programme uses carefully designed auctions to allocate publicly owned land to private developers[221].
