Chapter 4: The World Values Benchmark 182

Figure 27: Results from Q167 Do you believe in Hell? The baseline is the model. Q184: Is Abortion ever justifiable? On the original WVS, this was a 10-point justifiability scale; here, responses were collapsed into binary categories (justifiable / not justifiable) for comparability. PaLM consistently weighted “not justifiable” more strongly. In the graphs, this shows up as low KL divergence with the US distribution (also restrictive), but larger divergence from the Netherlands (where abortion is widely accepted) and Nigeria (where abortion is largely illegal). In other words, the model’s restrictive stance mirrors US tendencies but for very different cultural reasons than those underlying Nigerian responses: an important reminder that low divergence can mask qualitatively different alignments.
