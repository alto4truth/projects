Model capacity influences continual learning dynamics in our experimental setting. The smaller Phi-4-Mini
model benefits most from COMPASS-ECDA’s targeted preservation strategy, attributable to insufficient capacity to store essential information via DoRA, as it suffers from the largest regressions in backward transfer
when using methods such as naive fine-tuning. LLaMA-3.1 demonstrates similar patterns with enhanced stability, while Qwen2.5’s stronger multilingual pretraining amplifies COMPASS-ECDA’s effectiveness, yielding
the highest absolute performance across all periods.
The consistent pattern across architectures, aggregated over multiple target languages and averaged over 3
randomseeds, validatesthegeneralizabilityofCOMPASS-ECDA’sapproach. Completetemporaltrajectories
and statistical comparisons for all models are provided in Appendix D.
5.6 COMPASS adaptation results in generalized multilingual benefits
The benefits of COMPASS generalize across other evaluation tasks, even without distribution matching (i.e.,
when cluster-level weights in COMPASS are not a dominant factor). The magnitude of improvement is most
pronounced for languages that were initially poorly performing (MGSM) and on tasks that are similar to
the fine-tuning objective (XQuAD) 8.
