4.5.1 RQ2.1. Mutation at Different Circuit Depths.The mean accuracies on MNIST are shown in
Figure 3(a). By varying the depth range on the same QNN, we observe that mutations introduced
at shallow depths (0–50%) tend to produce greater performance degradation compared to deeper
ones (75–100%), as indicated by the lower accuracies at shallow depths in general. For example, on
QCL with RGA operator, shallow mutation at 0–25% reduces accuracy to 90.25% (a 9.75% drop),
whereas deep mutation at 75–100% causes only a 3.18% drop. As shown in Table 4, shallow-depth
mutations achieve higher KRs and lower NRs, meaning that more killable or trivial mutants are
generated and thus have a greater impact on the model performance.
