3.3 Theory–experiment independence
A natural design instinct is to maximize context sharing between the mathematical and experimental tracks.
In our experience, this improves local alignment but weakens both tracks (Lesson 3 in Section 2).
The non-obvious design: sharelesscontext, getbetterpapers. The theory and experiment tracks share
an explicit decomposition (track_decomposition.json) and common contribution claims, but they do
notcontinuously share unrestricted working context. Instead, they exchange information at controlled
synchronization points—especially before synthesis and at the beginning of a new cycle.
