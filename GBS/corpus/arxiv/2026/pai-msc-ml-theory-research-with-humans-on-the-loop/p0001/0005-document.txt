Producing only rigorously established work is not a methodological preference—it is anethics of research. If
researchers will increasingly rely on AI systems, then the highest-leverage place to enforce that ethic isinside
those systems: bias the tools toward establishing results solidly, and establishment scales with adoption.
pAI/MScis our attempt to do exactly this. System prompts and control flows are designed to make novelty
claims explicit, stress-test them against competing explanations, align mathematics with experiments, and
demand intervention experiments when causal language is involved. A field with more papers is not bad
per se—provided those papers push the knowledge boundary. We believe optimizing for quality the AI tools
that researchers use is the intervention most likely to move the needle.
