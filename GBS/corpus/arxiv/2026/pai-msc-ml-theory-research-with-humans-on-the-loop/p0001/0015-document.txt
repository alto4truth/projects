2 Engineering Lessons and Design Requirements
This section has two goals. First, it makes explicit the main engineering difficulties we encountered while
buildingpAI/MSc, so that future work can attack them directly rather than rediscover them piecemeal.
Second, it serves as guidance for users who may customize or extend the system: it documents which design
choices were responses to concrete workflow failures, which problems we believe are only partially solved,
and which interface contracts should be preserved if one wants the pipeline to remain reliable. We present
these lessons in the same order in which they typically appear during a research-to-paper run.
2.1 Difficulties Encountered
1. Ideation quality improved when planning became debate rather than monologue.One
of our earliest observations was that single-agent ideation tended to converge too quickly to generic or
aesthetically plausible project plans. Quality improved substantially when the early planning phase was
reformulated as a structured debate among multiple agents rather than a single agent trying to ideate,
justify, and plan everything at once. This was also oserved, but implemented differently by Du et al. [52]
