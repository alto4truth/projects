2. Give agents explicit, competing objectives.A second related observation was that debate alone
was not enough: each agent needed an explicit goal. In practice, the quality of the resulting research plans
increased when we assigned different roles and incentives to different ideation agents. In the current design,
these roles include one agent pushing for timely and practitioner-relevant questions, one pushing for rigorous
and potentially novel mathematical theory, and one pushing for the strongest narrative arc together with
the right alignment or disalignment with existing folklore. This does not solve ideation in general, but it
consistently produced better decompositions and more informative disagreements than a single planner.
