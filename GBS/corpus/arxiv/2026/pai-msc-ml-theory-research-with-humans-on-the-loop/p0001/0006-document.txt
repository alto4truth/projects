Why Academic Research Is Harder Than Current Agentic Search
How is our setting different fromAutoResearch[43] and related systems?Not mainly because we target
theory, but because academic research offers much weaker objective feedback. Many recent agentic successes
depend on three favorable conditions:
1.Bounded domain.AutoResearch edits a single file,train.py.
2.Short evaluation loops.Each AutoResearch run is capped at five minutes.
3.Automatic objectives.AutoResearch optimizes validation bits-per-byte; MLAgentBench [44] likewise studies executable ML tasks with clear goals and automatic evaluation.
In more open-ended settings, evaluation itself becomes part of the problem. The AI Scientist [45], as
pAI/MSc, relies on simulated review and an automated reviewer; Agent Laboratory [46] allows human
guidance and finds that it improves research quality; and MLR-Bench [47] uses an LLM judge with structured
rubrics.
Academic research, especially theory, weakens all three assumptions. The search space is open-ended, significance may become clear only years later, as in delayed recognition and “sleeping beauties” [48], and no
