Hierarchical Depth and Recursive ScalingThe
recursive nature of CFGs poses a significant challenge for hierarchical state-tracking. By evaluating LLMs acrossMax Nesting Depths( D∈
{2,5,10,15,20} ), we observe a sharp, non-linear
decay across all metrics (Figure 2). At D= 2 , the
model achieves a moderate SVR of 42.0% and an
SCR of 39.0%, indicating relatively stable structural alignment at shallow levels. However, as the
depth increases to 20, SVR drops to 10.0%, while
SCR nearly vanishes at 0.50%. This widening gap
between syntax and semantics, where the CSCR
falls from 92.8% to a negligible 5.0%, suggests
that while LLMs can occasionally produce locally
parseable code at high depths, they almost entirely
lose the global state-tracking required to faithfully
