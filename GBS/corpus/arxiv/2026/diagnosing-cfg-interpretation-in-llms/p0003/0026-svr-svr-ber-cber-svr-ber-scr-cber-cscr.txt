Semantic DriftThe most subtle failure mode
is semantic drift, where the generated code is behaviorally functional but semantically non-aligned.
This is primarily driven by two phenomena: (1)
AST flattening biasin arithmetic and boolean contexts, where LLMs prematurely evaluate expressions into simplified results. (2)Autoregressive
Echoing, where the model loses its position within
deep nested sequences and redundantly duplicates
entire logical blocks. These failures reveal that
LLMs often prioritize simulative success over structural fidelity, acting as independent executors rather
than faithful in-context grammar interpreters.
