Step 3: Weakest methodology.Among peer-reviewed
entries, Songet al.[C5] is most vulnerable to methodological
criticism:
• Sample size adequacy:Validated on a per-paper selection
of CNN models; no systematic sampling across architecture
families or scales.
• Control for confounds:Feature-space geometry varies fundamentally between CNNs (local receptive fields), ViTs
(global attention), and decoder-only LLMs (sequential token embeddings). The metric’s inter/intra-class distance
ratio may conflate architecture-specific geometry with genuine robustness signal.
• Replicability:Code availability is partial; key implementation details (layer selection heuristic, centroid computation
for imbalanced classes) are underspecified.
• Transparency of reporting:Confidence intervals on the
correlation between RDI and attack-based rankings are
not reported; the ∼30× speedup claim lacks wall-clock
variance across hardware configurations.
Criterion most clearly failed:Control for confounds—the
CNN-only validation leaves the metric’s validity for the fastestgrowing model families (ViTs, LLMs) entirely unsubstantiated.
