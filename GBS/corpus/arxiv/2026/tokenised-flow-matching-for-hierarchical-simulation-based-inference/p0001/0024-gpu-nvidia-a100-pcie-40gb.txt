Hierarchical parameters.Global parameters are θg = (logβ scale,logµ,logQ in), where βscale
rescales a baseline stiffness profile,µ is blood viscosity, andQin sets inflow amplitude. We treat each
patientas a site s∈ { 1, . . . , ns}. Each patient has terminal outlet parameters at the four terminal
vessels, i.e. ηs = (logR T,s,1 ,logC T,s,1 , . . . ,logR T,s,4 ,logC T,s,4) ∈R 8 (implemented as Windkessel
R2 andC, withR 1 fixed).
Priors.We use LogNormal priors on global parameters: logβ scale ∼ N (logβ mean, 0.32), logµ∼
N (log 0.004, 0.22), logQ in ∼ N (log 85, 0.22). Local priors are centred around structured-tree-inspired
reference values( RT,ref (s), CT,ref (s))with anti-correlated perturbations to keep the outlet time
constantτ s =R T,s CT,s approximately stable:
logR T,s,o = logR T,ref (o) + 0.4ϵs,o,logC T,s,o = logC T,ref (o)−0.4ϵ s,o, ϵ s,o ∼ N(0,1),
for patientsand outlet indexo∈ {1, . . . ,4}.
Observations and noise model.We condition only on the four flow traces (one per terminal
vessel), each after simulating 5 cardiac cycles and resampling the final cycle toNt points, giving
ys ∈R 4Nt for patient s. We use heteroscedastic Gaussian noise on each flow trace with standard
deviationσ s,o =εmax t |Qs,o(t)|(withε= 0.05).
LF sampling in the CFD setting.Stage 1 trains the per-site neural surrogateqϕl(ys |θ g, ηs)
using per-patient tuples, where a full-network run for patients yields the four outlet flow traces
comprising ys. Stage 2 generates multi-site training observations by samplingy∼q ϕl(y|θ g, η)and
trains the posterior estimatorqϕp(θg, η|y)using a tokenised estimator as described in Section 2.1.
