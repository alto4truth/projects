4 Discussion
The choice of factorisation strategy depends on the scale of local parameterisation relative to simulation budget. Standard NPE remains practical when the number of sites is small. Posterior
factorisation methods offer a middle ground, achieving good sample efficiency with limited error
propagation at moderate scales. LF sampling provides a distinct advantage: when local parameterisation is large and simulations are expensive, the savings from single-site training offset the
approximation error in the per-site neural surrogate. The "Direct" ablation in Appendix A.5 shows
that this surrogate approximation error is small on most benchmark tasks, and the matched "PF"
ablation shows that swapping to LF while holding the architecture, optimiser, and schedule fixed
can lead to more efficient estimation on most benchmark tasks, indicating that LF contributes
sample-efficiency gains distinct from those of the tokenised flow-matching architecture its
