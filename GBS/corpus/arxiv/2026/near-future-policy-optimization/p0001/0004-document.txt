1 Introduction
Reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) has become a core post-training recipe for reasoning
models [8, 20, 31], but pure on-policy exploration faces two structural limits: early training suffers from
sparse correct trajectories, whereas later training often converges to a plateau after the rollout distribution
narrows [33, 36]. A natural response is to enrich the learning signal by mixing in auxiliary trajectories from
other sources, moving from pure on-policy updates to a mixed-policy regime. Recent work has explored
this direction along two lines: importing stronger traces from outside the current policy through off-policy
demonstrations [25], expert prefixes [9], or interleaved supervised corrections [5, 16]; or reusing successful
trajectories produced during training itself, as in experience replay and restart-style methods [35, 36]. Yet
both lines face a common tension. External trajectories carry rich signal but diverge in reasoning patterns
from the current policy, making them difficult to internalize. Replayed trajectories stay close to the on-policy
