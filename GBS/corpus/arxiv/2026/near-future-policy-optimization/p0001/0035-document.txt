5 Related Work
5.1 Mixed-Policy RLVR
RLVR has become the dominant post-training recipe for reasoning models [8, 20, 31], and while GRPO
variants such as DAPO, GSPO, and SAPO improve the on-policy optimization [4, 6, 20, 26–28, 30, 31, 39],
a growing line of work shows that the choice of trajectory source matters just as much. One category
imports stronger supervision from outside the current policy: LUFFY mixes external teacher traces into
training [25], Prefix-RFT injects expert prefixes [9], and ReLIFT, SRFT, and TRAPO interleave RL with
targeted supervised updates [5, 16, 23]. These methods provide high-quality trajectories but introduce a large
distributional gap. The other category reuses successful trajectories from training itself: RePO and ExGRPO
maintain replay buffers of historical successes [11, 35], and RLEP restarts training with replayed experience
from a stronger seed policy [36]. These stay closer to the current policy but are bounded by the quality of
earlier checkpoints, and stored trajectories drift away from the evolving policy over time.
