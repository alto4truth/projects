6 Conclusion and Future Work
This paper explores a central question: what kind of auxiliary learning signal yields the greatest benefit when
introduced into RLVR? We propose a core idea—Near-future Policy Optimization (NPO)—where the model’s
temporal future self guides its present self, thereby obtaining signals that are sufficiently strong (highQ )
and readily learnable (lowV). We demonstrate the superiority and generality of NPO both theoretically
and empirically. We instantiate this idea by mixing near-future policy trajectories into the current rollout
