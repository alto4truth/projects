Intuitively, the optimization process itself continuously produces such off-policy trajectory candidates: every
later checkpoint along the same training run is a more optimized version of the current policy. As the step
distance∆grows, Q increases (more optimization) but so doesV (more parameter drift), making∆a natural
dial for theQ–V trade-off. Anear-futurecheckpoint occupies the high-S region: far enough ahead to offer a
meaningful Q gain, yet close enough to keepV small. This motivatesNear-FuturePolicyOptimization (NPO),
