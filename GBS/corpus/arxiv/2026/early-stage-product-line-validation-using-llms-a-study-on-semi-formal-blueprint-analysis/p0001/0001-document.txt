Abstract
We study whether Large Language Models (LLMs) can performfeature model analysis operations(AOs) directly onsemi-formal textual
blueprints, i.e., concise constrained-language descriptions of feature
hierarchies and constraints, enabling early validation in Software
Product Line scoping. Using 12 state-of-the-art LLMs and 16 standard AOs, we compare their outputs against the solver-based oracle
FLAMA. Results show thatreasoning-optimized models(e.g., Grok 4
Fast Reasoning, Gemini 2.5 Pro) achieve 88–89% average accuracy
across all evaluated blueprints and operations, approaching solver
correctness. We identify systematic errors in structural parsing and
