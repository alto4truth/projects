5.3.6. Interpretability analysis
Although DAIRE is implemented as a lightweight feed-forward ANN,
its ability to distinguish between different spoofing attacks is explained
by the structured nature of CAN message encoding. Each vehicle function (e.g., speed, engine RPM, throttle position, gear state) is represented by fixed Arbitration IDs and specific payload byte positions.
Spoofing attacks typically manipulate only those CAN fields related
to the targeted function, leading to consistent, class-specific statistical deviations in a limited subset of input features. For example,
