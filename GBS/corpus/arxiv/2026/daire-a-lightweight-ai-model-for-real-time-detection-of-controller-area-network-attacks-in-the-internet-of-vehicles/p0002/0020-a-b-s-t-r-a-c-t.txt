The lack of authentication and encryption in the CAN protocol
significantly exposes the network to various cyber threats (Yang et al.,
2023). Attackers can exploit these weaknesses by injecting fabricated
messages with forged Arbitration IDs, effectively spoofing legitimate
ECUs on the network. DoS attacks may be carried out by overwhelming
the bus with a stream of high-priority frames, especially those containing dominant 0 bits in the Arbitration Field, thereby obstructing normal
communication. Attackers tamper with the Data Field to change critical
payloads, such as changing speedometer readings or sensor values,
posing serious risks to vehicle functionality and passenger safety.
