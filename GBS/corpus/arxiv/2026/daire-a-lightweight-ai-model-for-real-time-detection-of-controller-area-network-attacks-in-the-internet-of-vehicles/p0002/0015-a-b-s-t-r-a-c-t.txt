Despite their powerful learning capabilities, these architectures often require substantial computational resources and memory, making
them less suitable for resource-constrained environments such as embedded automotive systems. In contrast, the DAIRE model utilizes a
lightweight ANN to ensure low-latency, lightweight deployment in realtime IoV scenarios. While deeper models like CNNs and LSTMs offer
performance advantages in some domains, our empirical evaluation
demonstrates that a lightweight ANN can achieve competitive accuracy
in CAN attack detection without the overhead of complex architectures.
This trade-off between complexity and efficiency makes our ANN-based
DAIRE model a practical choice for IoV environments.
