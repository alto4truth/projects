4.1 The Scaling Hypothesis for Value-Aligned AI
The structural definition of the value alignment problem clarifies that every instance of misalignment is indexed
to a particular principal or set of principals. Some principals’ goals (for example, those of developers, regulators,
and consumers) may converge. Still, in many cases, they diverge in ways that are decisive for assessing whether a
system is genuinely aligned. A model may therefore be well aligned relative to one principal while simultaneously
