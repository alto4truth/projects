problem(also known as an “agency dilemma” or an “incentive problem”). In these contexts, a principal delegates
authority to an agent to act on her behalf, yet the agent’s actions may not always reflect the principal’s best
interests. This framework has become increasingly relevant in the AI literature, where scholars have drawn
explicit parallels between value alignment and the principal-agent problem [34, 49]. Recent work emphasises the
structural similarities between these domains, framing value alignment as a multi-agent coordination problem
