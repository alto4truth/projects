This reconceptualisation, therefore, situates alignment within a broader frame of socio-technical governance. AI
systems are embedded in networks of labour, data extraction, and environmental cost. These systems’ “objectives”
are operationalised not only through code but through economic incentives, regulatory regimes, and the social
hierarchies in which they are deployed. To treat alignment as an engineering problem alone is to ignore the
upstream power structures that determine who gets to specify objectives and who bears the risks when they fail.
