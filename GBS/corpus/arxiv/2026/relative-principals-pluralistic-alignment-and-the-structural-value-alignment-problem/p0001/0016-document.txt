When information between the principal and the agent is symmetric (in the idealised economic model), the
principal can theoretically design a contract that induces the agent to act in her best interest, even when their goals
diverge. Conversely, even when the principal and the agent have identical incentives, informational asymmetries
can cause the agent to (inadvertently) act in a way that the principal does not want. Thus, in the economic
