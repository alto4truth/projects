These socio-technical dynamics provide further motivation for the scaling hypothesis described above. As AI
systems scale in capability and deployment, the set of affected stakeholders expands far beyond the immediate
developers and users. Systems trained and deployed by a small number of organisations may influence millions of
individuals across jurisdictions, cultures, and regulatory environments. The resulting expansion of the principal
set intensifies pluralistic disagreement about acceptable uses, risks, and benefits. In other words, scaling AI
