CHAIR.Apart from binary “Yes-or-No” questions, we
adopt CHAIR [38] to evaluate hallucinations in the image captioning task. Specifically, CHAIR operates by
computing the proportion of all objects mentioned in the
caption that do not exist in the ground-truth annotations.
CHAIR comprises two main metrics, including CHAIR I
and CHAIR S that assess instance-level and sentence-level
hallucinations, respectively. They are formulated as follows:
CHAIRI = |{hallucinated objects}|
|{all mentioned objects}| ,
CHAIRS = |{captions w/ hallucinated objects}|
|{all captions}| ,
(2)
where lower values indicate fewer hallucinations. Besides,
we also consider the F1 score to assess the richness and the
accuracy of the generated captions.
