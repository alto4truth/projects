4.1. Experimental Setup
4.1.1. Baselines
We build our R-CoV upon several popular opensource LVLMs, including mPLUG-Owl (mplug-owl-llama7b) [53], LLaV A (llava-v1.5-7b) [28], MiniGPT-4 (vicuna13b) [59], and Qwen2.5-VL (qwen2.5-vl-7b-instruct) [3].
We refer to the base LVLMs as vanilla. In addition, we
also compare R-CoV with other advanced hallucination
detection and mitigation methods, including (i) methods
based on careful instruction tuning or retraining (e.g., LRVInstruction [27], LURE [58]), and (ii) methods heavily relying on external expert models (e.g., SelfCheckGPT [32],
LogicCheckGPT [49]).
4.1.2. Benchmarks
We evaluate our R-CoV on several widely used hallucination benchmarks, including POPE [25], MME [13],
CHAIR [38], and GPT-4o assisted evaluation.

designed in the VQA paradigm. Specifically, it evaluates hallucinations by querying LVLMs with the questions
in the form of “Is there a/an <object> in the
