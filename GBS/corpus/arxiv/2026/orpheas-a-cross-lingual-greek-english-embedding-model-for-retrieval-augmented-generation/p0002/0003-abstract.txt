1 Introduction
Information retrieval (IR) in bilingual settings introduces compounded challenges, which extend beyond those encountered in monolingual systems. When one of the languages is morphologically complex, such as Greek [Koutsikakis
et al., 2020], inflectional variation produces multiple surface realizations of identical semantic concepts, undermining the practical applicability of conventional IR architectures based on lexical matching algorithms [Kadhim, 2019].
