3.3 Proactive Reinforcement Learning-based Retrieval
Within the online evolution framework, the agent must learn when retrieval actively improves outcomes versus when
current knowledge suffices. Let zt =1[a t =RETRIEVE(q t)] denote whether a retrieval action is executed at stept. The
core difficulty is that outcome-level rewards signal task success or failure but cannot isolate whether a specific retrieval
decision at a particular step was necessary or beneficial. When an episode fails, the agent cannot determine whether
retrieval was unnecessary, mistimed, or too vague. To learn proactive retrieval control, we require step-level supervision
that directly answers: did this retrieval actively improve the trajectory, or would the agent have succeeded without it?
