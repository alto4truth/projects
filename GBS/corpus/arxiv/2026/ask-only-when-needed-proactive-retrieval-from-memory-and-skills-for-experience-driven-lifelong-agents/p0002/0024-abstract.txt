Here τ<tb and τ≥tb denote the ordered prefix and suffix subsequences of τ, respectively, and ⊕ denotes sequence
concatenation. The rollout τ ret preserves the retrieval decision and its subsequent context, whereas τ no-ret explores
alternative actions from the same prefix by temporarily suppressing the retrieval action at tb. Because both rollouts
share the same history before the branching point, the divergence between them provides a precise comparative signal:
if τ ret outperforms τ no-ret, the retrieval was proactive and necessary; if not, the retrieval was passive or redundant.
This paired-branch comparison directly isolates the utility of the specific retrieval decision, enabling step-level process
rewards for proactive control.
