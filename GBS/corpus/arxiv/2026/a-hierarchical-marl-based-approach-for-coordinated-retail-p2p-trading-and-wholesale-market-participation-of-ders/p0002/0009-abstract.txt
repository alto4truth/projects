Data-driven methods, specifically deep reinforcement
learning(DRL),haverecentlygainedattentioninenergy
management and market applications [27]. Using the
wealth of data, DRL agents could discover optimal
decision-making policies from real or simulated experience,learningtomanageuncertaintieswithoutrequiring
explicit system models [28]. DRL has been applied to
optimize residential energy trading [29], EV charging
strategies [30], and building energy management [31,
32]. However, scaling single-agent RL to a multi-agent
context (MARL) is challenging due to large state-action
spaces, non-stationarity, and agent heterogeneity. Current MARL falls into three categories: (i) Independent
Learning [33, 34, 35], which can lead to uncoordinated
outcomes and unstable training; (ii) Centralized Critic
[36, 37], which mitigates non-stationarity but imposes
computation and communication costs, and raises privacy issues; and (iii) Value Decomposition and Policy
Sharing,suitableforcooperationbutlesseffectivewhen
agents have divergent objectives [38].
