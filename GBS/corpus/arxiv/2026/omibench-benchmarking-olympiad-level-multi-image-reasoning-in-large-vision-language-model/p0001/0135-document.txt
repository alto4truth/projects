Sampling stability and statistical significance.We report the mean and standard deviation of both
GPTScore and ACC across three independent sampling runs of Gemini-3-Pro-Preview, evaluated by Gemini2.5-flash and GPT-5-mini. As shown in Table 5, the standard deviations remain small: for GPTScore, all
subject-level deviations are below1.3% and total-level Std is below0.5%; for ACC, fluctuations are at most
∼1.3% and total-level Std is below0.3%. These variations are much smaller than the effective gains reported
in our paper for techniques such as long-CoT thinking, sequential scaling, and in-context learning, which
exceed 5% and in most cases exceed10%. Moreover, paired comparisons for Qwen-VL-32B-Thinking vs.
Instruct, Qwen-VL-32B-Instruct sequential scaling (1 vs. 16 samples), and Qwen-VL-32B-Instruct textual ICL
(0-shot vs. 3-shot) over five repeated runs yieldp-values all below0.05, indicating that our conclusions are
statistically stable.
