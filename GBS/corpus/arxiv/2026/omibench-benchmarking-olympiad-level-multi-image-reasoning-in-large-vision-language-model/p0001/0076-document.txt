Fine-Grained Topic Annotation.Because the fine-grained topics are unknown a priori, we first prompt
GPT-4o to perform open-ended topic analysis for each sample and to generate classification labels in the
form {CLASS: X}. Specifically, the prompts are as follows:
Fine-Grained Topic Annotation Prompt
You are an expert annotator for multi-image, multi-discipline Olympiad-level problems. Your task is to
assign the most specific sub-category label to each problem.
Each sample may contain:
•One or more images (diagrams, experiment setups, screenshots, etc.).
•Optional text (problem statement, description, notes).
Annotation rules:
•Choose the label by the core method and main concept, not by surface story.
• Always pick the most fine-grained label available (do not annotate coarse-grained subjects like
"Optics").
•If it mixes multiple topics, use "#" as a divider.
• Use all images and text jointly; visual cues (rays, lenses, optical axes, diagrams) are as important
as text.
Your output must be exactly one label string by {CLASS: X}. Do not output explanations or reasoning.
