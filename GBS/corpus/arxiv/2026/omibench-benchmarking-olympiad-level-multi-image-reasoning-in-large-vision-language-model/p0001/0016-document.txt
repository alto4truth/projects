Step 3: Quality ControlTo ensure dataset quality, we employ a dual-review protocol in which every problem receives at least one independent audit, complemented by weekly random sampling of 5% of examples
for regression testing on key metrics, error rate, text-image alignment, and solution accuracy. Further, audit
feedback is introduced to drive iterative updates to annotation guidelines and targeted retraining, forming a
closed-loop quality assurance process that maintains a high-fidelity multimodal competition problem corpus.
Step 4: Classification Labeling.OMIBench problems in biology, chemistry, mathematics, and physics fall
into two categories: open-ended and multi-choices. The combined Olympiad and high-stakes examination
corpus covers a wide range of subfields, as shown in Figure 2. We first use GPT-4o to generate preliminary
topic labels, and then manually assign final topic to ensure consistency and correctness across the corpus.
