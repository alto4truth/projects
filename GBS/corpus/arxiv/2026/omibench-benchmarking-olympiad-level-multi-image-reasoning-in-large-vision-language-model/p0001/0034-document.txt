ScienceQA [39], MMMU [68], and M3CoT [11] extend evaluation across multiple disciplines [31]. OlympiadBench [24] targets advanced scientific reasoning through Olympiad problems in mathematics and physics,
while Physics Big [53] provides a large-scale collection of physics competition problems for evaluating
quantitative problem solving.
Recent work has also examined multi-image understanding in LVLMs. Mementos [60] studies narrative
and temporal reasoning over image sequences; MC-Bench [65] evaluates multi-context visual grounding;
and MANTIS [30] offers interleaved vision–language instruction-tuning data with broad, general-domain
difficulty. Although these benchmarks effectively test cross-image reference, alignment, and commonsense
composition, they mainly emphasize narrative or perceptual integration rather than the tight semantic and
quantitative coupling required by competition-level scientific problems.

focus largely on single-image settings or relatively shallow multi-image perception, and seldom capture
