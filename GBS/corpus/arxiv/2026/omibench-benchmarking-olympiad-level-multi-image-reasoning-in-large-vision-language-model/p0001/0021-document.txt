OMIBench needs stronger awareness of the information flow across images.Generally, MMIU [42]
targets basic multi-image understanding, whereas OMIBench demands complex reasoning across images.
To confirm this, we compare model performance. As shown in Figure 3 (a), OMIBench shows moderate
Spearman correlation with MMIU (< 0.7): it links to multi-image tasks but Olympiad-level difficulty alters
model rankings. Beyond this, we further examine multi-image information integration. As shown in Figure 3
(b), single-image accuracy reaches 40%, dropping below 15% for inputs with≥6 images. Restricting
instances to one image (Figure 3 (c)) causes at least a 10% absolute performance drop versus using all
images, revealing LVLMs’ struggles with cross-image integration.
