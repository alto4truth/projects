To decouple visual perception capabilities from reasoning engines, we developed a specific error taxonomy
comprising five distinct categories: (1)Visual Perception Failures, where the model misinterprets explicit
visual semantics; (2)Cross-Image Association Failures, indicating an inability to synthesize information
across multi-view inputs; (3)Logical Reasoning Fallacies, covering invalid deductions and calculation
errors despite correct perception; (4)Instruction Comprehension Biases, regarding format or constraint
violations; and (5)Otherfor hallucinations or uncategorized failures. This taxonomy allows us to diagnose
whether performance bottlenecks stem from the vision encoder, the cross-modal alignment, or the LLMs’
reasoning core.
