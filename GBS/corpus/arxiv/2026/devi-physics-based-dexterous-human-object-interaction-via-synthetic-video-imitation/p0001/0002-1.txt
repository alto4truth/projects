4 DeVI: Dexterous Video Imitation
Given an initial scene including a 3D human, represented as a SMPL-X pose, and a target 3D object,
our goal is to learn a policy π to generate physically plausible HOI motion to manipulate the target
object in simulation, following the action specified by a text prompt. Our core idea is to leverage a
video diffusion model [49] to synthesize 2D videos, from which we extract hybrid imitation targets,
including 3D human motion and 2D object trajectories, to train the humanoid control policy.
In this paper, we focus on dexterous hand-object manipulation in tabletop scenarios. We initialize a
scene S={H,O} , where H denotes the human parameterized by SMPL-X [33], and O represents
the object defined as follows:
H={β,θ b,ϕ b,τ b,θ h}(2)
O={ϕ o,τ o},(3)
where β∈R 10, θb ∈R 21×3, θh ∈R 30×3, ϕh ∈SO(3) , and τ h ∈R 3 are the SMPL-X parameters
of human shape, body pose, hand pose, global body root orientation, and global body root translation,
respectively. The ϕo ∈SO(3) and τ o ∈R 3 are the object’s global orientation and translation. We
define MO(ϕo,τ o) as a mapping from the object state to the transformed object mesh. We use a
similar function MSMPLX(H) to obtain the SMPL-X mesh from the parameters. In this section, we
consider the single object case for simplicity, but our framework is not restricted to it.
We first present our method to synthesize an HOI video from the input scene following the prompt
instruction (Sec. 4.1). From the video, we obtain hybrid imitation targets (Sec. 4.2). Then, we present
our novel hybrid tracking reward (Sec. 4.3) for learning the control policy. An overview of our
method is shown in Fig. 2.
