5.3 Case 1 Hold Behavior
The two-index framework’s Case 1 (Equation 3) fires when a
downward pricing shift would require retracting a previous
offer; the broker holds at its last offer instead. In the evaluation, Case 1 fires in only 3.2% of two-index negotiations
(680 of 21,000), with a mean of 3.7 holds per affected negotiation. Holds concentrate in narrow spreads: 23.3% of 𝑆=1%
negotiations trigger at least one hold, dropping to 7.8% at
𝑆=2% and 0% above 𝑆=7%. Holds arise almost exclusively
from downward shifts (676 of 680 cases) and correlate with
negotiation failure: only 29.7% of hold negotiations reach
agreement, compared to 65.1% overall. This confirms that
the monotonicity guarantee comes at the cost of reduced
flexibility after downward repricing, but the cost is confined
to narrow spreads where margins leave the least room for
concession.
