A.6 Proof of Theorem 6
Following the proof strategy of Miller (2023) for simple mixture models, we prove the result by
leveraging the famous posterior consistency theorem by Doob (1949).7 However, the latter requires a
stronger notion of identifiabilitywith respect to SMoGE parameters. This, despite the identifiability
of SMoGE densitieswith respect to mixing measures, does not hold onΘ∞ because mixing measures
are invariant under (i) permutations of expert labels, and (ii) mergers and separations of equal
mixing measure atoms. Therefore, we first construct a restricted space˜Θ∞ where the assumptions of
Doob’s theorem (measurability of the model and parameter identifiability) hold; then we show that
