2.3 CQS masking
Subsequences constructed by CQS Divide exhibit structured overlap. While this overlap is essential
to ensure full coverage of all-token interactions, it introduces redundancy: since each chunk appears
in l subsequences, interactions within the corresponding chunk-pair regions are computed l times.
Directly aggregating these results would lead to incorrect computation results. To ensure correctness,
each subsequence must be assigned a pre-defined responsibility over the whole attention matrix.
Redundant contributions must be masked out and this necessitates the CQS mask. Importantly,
overlap occurs only along the main diagonal at the chunk level, or intra-chunk interactions, rather
