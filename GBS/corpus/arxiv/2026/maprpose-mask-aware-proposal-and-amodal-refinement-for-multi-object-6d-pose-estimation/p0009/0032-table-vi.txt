As shown in Table III, our method achieves a single-object
inference latency of 0.05 s on an NVIDIA RTX 4090, representing a24 × speedupover the 1.2 s required by FoundationPose.
This significant latency reduction is primarily attributed to
two key architectural optimizations: the MAPP module prunes
the initial sampling space down to only B= 7 high-quality
hypotheses, and the amodal mask prediction in AMPR provides
geometrically consistent initialization that substantially reduces
the number of refinement iterations i required for convergence.
As shown in Table VI, our design exhibits superior scalability
in multi-object scenarios. By replacing sequential rendering
with a tensorized execution model, MAPRPose achieves 1.47
FPS for full-scene inference ( N= 5 ), a 43 × throughput
improvement over FoundationPose (1.47 FPS vs. 0.034 FPS
per frame). Notably, as N increases, our framework can
saturate the GPU, elevating utilization from 70% to over 90%
and further boosting the inference steady-state to 1.50 FPS.
