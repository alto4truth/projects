To verify the contribution of the AMPR module to the
refinement process, we analyze the convergence behavior of
our model across varying iteration counts on the LINEMOD
dataset (see Fig. 6). A key observation is that our full model
exhibits a faster convergence rate compared to the variant
without amodal mask prediction (w/o AMPR). Specifically, our
approach achieves a peak ADD-0.1d accuracy of 99.8% within
only 4 iterations and maintains a stable performance plateau.
In contrast, the baseline lacking amodal guidance converges
more slowly, requiring at least 6 iterations to reach its lower
saturation point of 99.2%. This enhanced convergence efficiency
demonstrates that amodal mask prediction provides geometric
priors, reducing the total number of iterations—and thus the
computational overhead—required to achieve high-precision
alignment.

performance of our mask-aware refinement module. By integrating amodal mask completion with a recursive feedback
