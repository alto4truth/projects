3.2. Dataset and evaluation metrics
We evaluate gaze-following task on the 4D-OR [26] and
Team-OR [6] datasets. For 4D-OR, following the original
configuration, we use 6, 2 and 2 videos for training, validation, and testing, respectively. For Team-OR, we use 80% of
videos for training and the rest for testing. We use the same
evaluation metrics as in [31]: the heatmap area under the curve
(AUC), which considers each heatmap pixel as a confidence
score for the receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve,
and pixel L2, defined as the normalized Euclidean distance
between the predicted and ground truth gaze points.
