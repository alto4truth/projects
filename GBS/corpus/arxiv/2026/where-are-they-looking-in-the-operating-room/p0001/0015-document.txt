Specifically, we propose a self-supervised learning strategy to
train a spatial-temporal transformer encoder that encodes cliplevel gaze features.
For each video clip comprisingNframes, where each frame
containsPpersons (withPvarying across frames), we extract gaze features of sizeGfor each person from the gaze
decoder of the gaze-following model without generating gaze
heatmap. This process yields a sequence of gaze feature vectorsC ∈ R N×P×G over time. Subsequently, we apply the
spatial-temporal transformer encoder: for each frame, we pass
the gaze vectorsC s ∈ R P×G with head positions encoded
through a three-layer encoder to obtain a frame-level vector
from the[CLS]token, and then we pass these frame vectors
Ct ∈ R N×G through another three-layer encoder to obtain the
final clip-level featuresf g of sizeVfrom the[CLS]token.
