11. Extension to Video Relighting
GeoRelight is primarily designed for single-image relighting and reconstruction by repurposing the temporal dimensionTof a pretrained video DiT as a modality dimension
M. However, the framework can be naturally extended
to multi-modal video relighting with minimal architectural
modification.
Architecture ModificationThe key change is in the positional encoding. In our static model, we apply a shared
2D RoPE [31] (H×W) identically across allMmodalities
to ensure spatial alignment. For the video extension, we replace this with a shared 3D RoPE (T×H×W) across allM
modalities. This allows the model to reason about both spatial correlations across modalities and temporal consistency
across frames, while still maintaining per-modality spatial
alignment.
Preliminary ExperimentTo validate this approach, we
generated 4,000 synthetic human videos and trained a video
variant of GeoRelight. Due to computational constraints,
we use 17-frame videos at1280×832resolution, which
are compressed to3×160×104latents using the Cosmos
8×8×8causal V AE [27]. The model is initialized from our
trained static GeoRelight weights. Our preliminary results
show that this video extension achieves meaningful temporal consistency for multi-modal video relighting, confirming that the architecture supports this generalization. A full
exploration of video relighting and reconstruction is an exciting direction for future work. Furthermore, our disentangled geometry and appearance outputs could serve as
a foundation for downstream tasks such as 3D-consistent
avatar creation [41, 43] and controllable 3D human generation [42].
