The advantage of estimating geometry and intrinsics was already raised by the seminal work by Barron et al. [2] in the
context of intrinsic image decomposition, without considering relighting yet. More recently, UniRelight [9] introduced
a general-purpose framework that jointly estimates albedo
and synthesizes relit video in a single pass. By formulating this as a joint denoising problem in a video diffusion
model [7, 23], it implicitly reasons about scene structure.
While powerful, its joint estimation is limited to albedo,
leaving a critical gap in explicitly and jointly modeling the
3D geometry essential for consistent shading.
