2. Related Works
Image relighting and its related problems (e.g. intrinsic
decomposition) have a long tradition in computer vision,
going back to the work on intrinsic images by Barrow [3]
and the Retinex algorithm by Land [20]. We encourage the
reader to read recent surveys [8, 48] for a wide viewpoint
on intrinsic decomposition and human relighting, while we
focus on the latest work on relighting in the remainder.
2.1. Neural Rendering and Relighting
Recent generative models have approached monocular image relighting in several ways. NeuralGaffer [12] treats
relighting as an end-to-end 2D diffusion process, finetuning a model on a large synthetic dataset to relight any object without explicit intrinsic decomposition. IC-Light [47]
focuses on illumination harmonization, using diffusion to
relight a foreground object to match a new background.
LightLab [24] enables direct manipulation of existing light
sources in an image through diffusion models.
