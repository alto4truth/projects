Training budgets are adjusted to reflect the computational difference between the
enumerable and non-enumerable settings. In the 1-cycle setting, the full reward landscape can be precomputed and cached, so training scales well and could in principle be
extended further; in practice, models are trained for 1000 steps with a batch size of 16.
In the 2-cycle setting, simulator evaluations become the main bottleneck, so training is
limited to 2000 steps as a compromise between convergence and wall-clock cost. This
larger setting is therefore used not only to test scalability, but also to expose the practical limits of the current adaptation mechanism under realistic simulation-evaluation
constraints.
