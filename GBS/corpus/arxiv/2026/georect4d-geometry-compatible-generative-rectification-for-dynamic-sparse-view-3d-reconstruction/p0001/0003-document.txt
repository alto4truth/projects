1 Introduction
Reconstructing dynamic 3D scenes from sparse multi-view videos remains a fundamentally ill-posed problem. Without dense cross-view constraints, limited spatiotemporal cues cause optimization over explicit representations to converge to
degenerate configurations, including geometric collapse, floaters, trajectory drift,
and texture ambiguity. Such failures are more pronounced in dynamic scenes,
where motion and geometry are tightly coupled under insufficient supervision.
Solving this challenge is critical for practical 6-DoF free-viewpoint video (FVV)
systems in resource-constrained capture settings.
Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) [38] and their dynamic extensions [5,43,45,
59] have demonstrated high-fidelity novel view synthesis under dense multi-view
supervision, but typically incur substantial training and rendering costs. More
recently, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) [26] offers an explicit and efficient alternative with real-time rendering
