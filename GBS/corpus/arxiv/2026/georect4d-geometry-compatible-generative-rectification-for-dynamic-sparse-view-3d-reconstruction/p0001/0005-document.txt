Existing sparse dynamic 3DGS methods [20,51] still struggle to maintain geometric stability under extremely limited supervision.
More recently, generative priors have been introduced to hallucinate missing content under sparse views. Several works leverage 2D generative models or
diffusion-based refinement to enhance degraded renderings or augment pseudosupervision [34,62,64]. However, most of these approaches are demonstrated on
static scenes. In dynamic settings, naive integration of generative feedback often
introduces new instabilities, as shown in Fig. 1. This failure stems from two fundamental barriers: significant geometric collapse within under-constrained representations hindering plausible hallucination, and the inherent mismatch between
