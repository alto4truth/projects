To mitigate sparse-view ambiguity, prior work primarily focuses on constraining the solution space through additional regularization. NeRF-based approaches
incorporate geometric supervision such as depth or normals [58,72] and frequency regularization [24,67]. Within the 3DGS framework, depth-guided constraints [29,78] and dropout-based stabilization [41,66] improve robustness under
sparse inputs. While these techniques show promising results in static scenes, extending sparse reconstruction to dynamic scenarios remains significantly more
challenging due to motion-induced ambiguity and temporal error accumulation.
