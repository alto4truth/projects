1 INTRODUCTION
As machine learning architectures and optimization strategies have matured, the focus of the community has shifted toward the foundational element of model performance: data quality. Previous
work has demonstrated that “more” is not synonymous with “better” (Raffel et al., 2020; Penedo
et al., 2023). Building on this insight, recent efforts have shown that aggressively curating highquality subsets from web-scale corpora can match or exceed the performance of models trained on
far larger datasets (Li et al., 2025; Penedo et al., 2024; Messmer et al., 2025).
Historically, data curation relied on heuristic-based filtering (Nguyen et al., 2023; Raffel et al.,
2023; Rae et al., 2022; Penedo et al., 2023). However, the emergence of model-based classifiers
has enabled a more nuanced selection of semantically dense content. FineWeb-Edu (Penedo et al.,
2024) reached the performance of LLMs trained on350Btokens of raw English data using only
