For each language, we evaluate filtering strategies by training 1B parameter Apertus models on
their filtered outputs and benchmarking on language-appropriate tasks (detailed in Appendix G). We
rank methods by their performance on each individual benchmark, then compute the average rank
across all benchmarks for that language. A lower average rank indicates better overall performance.
This ranking approach is robust to scale differences across benchmarks and emphasizes consistency
across diverse evaluation tasks. We also include mean normalized accuracy as a metric, as it gives
us more quantitative insight for the performance gain of each method.
