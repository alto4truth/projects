I ADDITION OFMKC-E DATASETS.
To make our classifier highly multilingual beyond the coverage given by the Aya Collection (Singh
et al., 2024), we extend the MKC+ pool with additional datasets (resulting in the MKC-e data). A
hypothesis is that, beyond allowing us to train on more languages, this addition provides more topics
and diversity, which can improve the performance of our filtering. In order to test this, we train some
monolingual classifiers on the MKC-e data and evaluate them using the training of the 1B Apertus.
Results are displayed in Tables 16, 17, and 18. While the training on MKC-e improves results for
Spanish by almost 0.5% in terms of normalized accuracy, we find that the results are more nuanced
for French, where the improvement is of 0.1%. However, the Arabic MKC-e classifier seems to
perform worse than the “No filtering” baseline, which suggests adding this new data pool provides
mixed results depending on the targeted language.
