Figures B and 1 show the distribution of quality scores assigned to French documents by different
classifiers. Several patterns emerge:
•French and Romance classifiers(Fig B) exhibit similar bimodal distributions: most documents score near 0 (clear negatives) with a long tail toward 1 (clear positives). The 90th
percentile cutoffs are comparable (French: 0.045, Romance: 0.048).
•Nordic classifier(Fig 1) produces a markedly different distribution despite successfully
separating quality tiers. It applies a much stricter threshold (90th percentile: 0.027) and
assigns generally lower scores, yet still identifies high-quality content effectively.
•Romance without French(Fig B) shows intermediate behavior, with more score mass in
the middle range, suggesting less confident predictions.
