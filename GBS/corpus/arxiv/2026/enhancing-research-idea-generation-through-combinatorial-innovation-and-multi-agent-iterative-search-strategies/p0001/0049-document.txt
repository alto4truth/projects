The first compared the generated ideas with ideas from accepted ICLR 2025 papers, and the second compared the generated ideas with ideas from rejected ICLR 2025 papers. As shown in Table 3, accepted paper ideas achieved a significantly higher mean score (2.776, SD = 1.646) than the ideas generated by our framework (2.224, SD = 0.777), and the difference was highly significant (t = 10.390, p < 0.001). This result indicates that, although the generated ideas have reached a reasonable quality level, they still fall short of the average quality of accepted papers at a top-tier conference. At the same time, the relatively large standard deviation among accepted paper ideas suggests greater variation in the quality distribution of accepted submissions.
