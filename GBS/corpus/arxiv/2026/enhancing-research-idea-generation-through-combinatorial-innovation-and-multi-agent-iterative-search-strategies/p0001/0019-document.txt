7 3.1 Dataset Construction We select natural language processing (NLP) as the experimental domain for both methodological and practical reasons. First, NLP is a mature and highly active field with rapid publication cycles and substantial conceptual overlap across subfields, mak-ing it well suited for evaluating research idea generation, where redundancy and limited novelty are common challenges. Second, because large language models are primarily trained on textual data, NLP provides a natural setting for assessing their generative, reasoning, and evaluative capabilities while reducing confounding effects caused by modality mismatch. Third, the NLP community follows relatively standardized con-ventions in paper structure, methodological reporting, and evaluation, which facilitates controlled comparison between generated ideas and real conference submissions. The dataset in this study serves two purposes: generating initial research ideas and constructing background knowledge for the multi-agent system.
