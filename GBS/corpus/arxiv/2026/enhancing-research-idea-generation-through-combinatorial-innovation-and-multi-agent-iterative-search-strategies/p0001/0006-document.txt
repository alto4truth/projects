Second, although methods such as NOVA (Hu et al., 2025) improve the efficiency of knowledge retrieval through iter-ative search, they do not explicitly address how retrieved knowledge elements can be recombined across heterogeneous viewpoints to stimulate novel conceptual combina-tions. Third, current frameworks rarely simulate the collaborative nature of scientific discovery, in which research ideas are continuously refined through interactions among researchers with different expertise and cognitive perspectives. These limitations can be better understood through the lens of combinatorial inno-vation theory. Schumpeter (1964) proposed that innovation fundamentally arises from novel recombinations of existing elements, such as technologies, resources, or knowledge components.
