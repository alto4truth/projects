Protocol.We simulate increasing levels of label shift by subsampling the target batch to enforce different class-mixture
skew strengths. We report performance at three shift degrees (Table 3): from mild to severe label shift. In all of the
experiments, we use a constant number of labeled samples
so the decrease in accuracy is not due to a smaller batch size.
In every TTA method, the adaptation is unlabeled; only the
class mixture is manipulated. While for TENT, AdaBN and
ARM-BN, the accuracy decreases rapidly when label shift
increases, the performance for CS-ARM-BN remains robust. Moreover, we also compare to AdaBN and ARM-BN
including negative controls so that every method uses the
same amount of samples to compute the BN statistics, and
it can be seen that CS-ARM-BN still presents the highest
performance.
