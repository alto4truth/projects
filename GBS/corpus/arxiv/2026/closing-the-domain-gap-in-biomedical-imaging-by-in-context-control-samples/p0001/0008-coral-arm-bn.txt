Nevertheless, in this work, we show that these methods also
underperform in the presence of small batch sizes and under
label shifts. Both scenarios, a) small sample sizes and b)
labels shifts in a new experimental batch are very plausible,
because of the typical design of such bioimaging studies
(Hughes et al., 2011; Knowles and Gromo, 2003). However,
almost all biomedical experiments guarantee that a specific
type of samples will bepresent in every new experimental batch (i.e., domain):negative controls(Chandrasekaran
et al., 2023; Bray et al., 2017). Therefore, we propose to
exploit the presence of negative controls and use them as
context samples both during training and for inference of a
meta-learning model, showing that this approach makes the
performance of our method robust to small numbers of new
experimental batches and to shifts in the class distribution.
