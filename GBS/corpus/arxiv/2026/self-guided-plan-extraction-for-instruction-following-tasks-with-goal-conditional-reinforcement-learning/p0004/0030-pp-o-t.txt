Limitations
While SuperIgor demonstrates a scalable approach
to integrating language-model-based planning with
reinforcement learning, it has several limitations.
First, the method depends on the quality of the initial plan structure induced by the language model.
The ontology graph is generated fully automatically, without human supervision, which may lead
to redundant, inconsistent, or cyclic dependencies
between subtasks. In practice, we mitigate this issue empirically by evaluating different language
models and selecting those that produce stable and
coherent plan graphs (Appendix B.4); our experiments show that several mid-sized models already
perform well in this setting.Nevertheless, robustness to poorly structured plan spaces remains a
limitation of the current approach.
In addition, the interaction between plan quality and policy learning is not always interpretable.
While our validation protocol provides a clear relative preference signal between candidate plans
for the same instruction (via repeated evaluation
across seeds), it can still be difficult to diagnose
training failures at the system level. In particular, when learning stalls, it is unclear whether the
