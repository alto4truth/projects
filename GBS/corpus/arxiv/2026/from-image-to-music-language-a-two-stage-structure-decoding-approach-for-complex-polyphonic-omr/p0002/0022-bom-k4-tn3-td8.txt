Transition Matrix and Constrained Sampling
To prevent syntactically invalid sequences during autoregressive generation, Paraff applies a hard
logit maskat each decoding step, derived from a G×G binarytoken-group transition matrix T
(where G is the number of groups). Entry T[i][j] = 1 permits a token from group j to follow a token
from groupi;T[i][j] = 0forbids it.

Sampling then draws from the renormalised softmax over valid continuations only. Some illustrative
rules: K→TN (1, key precedes time-sig numerator); TN→TD (1, numerator before denominator);
TD→S (1, time sig followed by staff selector);TN→TN (0, two consecutive numerators forbidden).
This shifts grammatical correctness from a post-hoc filter into the sampling process itself, guaranteeing
that every generated sequence is parseable — an important property when Paraff output is piped into
downstream topology-extraction tools.
Role in Topology Training Data
Every valid Paraff sentence carries, by construction, the fields needed to assemble an event-topology
supervision sample:
•Voice assignment:VBboundaries partition events into monophonic voice sequences.
• Tick positions: voice-internal order and duration tokens (Dn/Dot/W*) determine each event’s
absolute tick.
•Staff membership: inlineS1/S2context tokens record cross-staff assignments.
• Structural attributes: beam markers (Bl/Br), tuplet markers (W*), and expressive markers
(E*) supply the ancillary fields needed by BeadSolver’s evaluation function.
A Paraff sentence can therefore be deterministically decoded into the (x,voices,tick,div) tuple
consumed by the BeadSolver model without additional annotation. At scale, an autoregressive
Paraff generator produces structurally diverse measures that collectively cover the multi-voice and
cross-staff patterns most challenging for automated regulation — precisely the structural domain
BeadSolver is designed to handle.
The Paraff implementation is open-sourced athttps://github.com/findlab-org/paraff.
