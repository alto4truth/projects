Group size distribution.Most groups have a similar number of measures, but anthologies and
collections create a long tail. Table 4 provides a compact summary.
Independent test set: lilypond-topology.For evaluation, we use an independent LilyPondbased test set summarized in data/test-overview.tex. It contains 107 scores and 5,317 measurelevel samples. For the direct spartito-versus-ground-truth comparison reported below, this corresponds
to 88,754 scored events after excluding boundary tokens and unmatched extras from the denominator.
The collection contains a large fraction of genuinely polyphonic writing: 96.3% of scores contain at
least two voices, 48.6% include cross-staff writing, 37.4% include grace notes, and 29.0% include
tuplets.
The composer distribution is intentionally diverse but not uniform: Bach, Chopin, and Czerny account
for about 79% of measures, while the remaining composers contribute smaller but stylistically distinct
subsets (see Table 5b). The set spans common meters, dominated by 4/4 and 3/4, with a meaningful
share of 2/4 and compound meters (see Table 5c). It is also mostly in major keys. The five most
common key signatures are C major (23), G major (17), F major (13), E major (11), and B-flat major
(10); 6 scores contain modulations. Complexity also varies by composer: Chopin contributes a higher
share of tuplets and grace notes, while Bach exhibits more cross-staff writing.
The original LilyPond sources of this dataset mainly come from MutopiaProject. Its structured
measure-level annotations are produced through LilyPond score rendering, SVG geometry analysis,
and cross-checking by multiple structure-solving algorithms. The final correctness of every measure
in the test set was verified manually.
Train-test independence.The rendered-source portion of training provides large-scale coverage
with realistic spatial layouts, while recognition-feedback samples reflect production error patterns.
The test set is independent from both sources and is drawn from a separate LilyPond corpus, which
makes it a meaningful generalization check (summarized in Table 6).
5.3 Metrics
We evaluate regulation at the measure level. Each method receives the same candidate event set from
the visual stage and must output a structured result: voice chains, tick positions, and duration-related
attributes.
