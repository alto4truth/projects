The algorithm must assign to every non-sentinel element:
1. an integerorder oi (consecutive integers mark the same voice; a gap of more than one starts
a new voice),
2. a tick offsett i ≥0,
3. a division classd i ∈ {0, . . . ,8}(whole through 256th) and dot countδ i ∈ {0,1,2},

The ordering and duration assignments together determine the voice structure and the tick layout;
scalar attributes are resolved deterministically from predispositions after search.
B.2 Three-Phase Action Sequence
Each search step handles exactly one event through three sub-decisions executed in sequence.
Pass. Choose the next element to enter the chain (or EOS to close the current voice). The posterior
distribution over candidates is the successor vector returned by BeadPicker. Selecting EOS
when residue remains triggers a new voice: the BOS sentinel is re-entered and BeadPicker
is re-queried with the updated prefix.
Division. Assign a division class to the newly chained element. The posterior is the element’s
predispositiondivisionVector.
Dots. Select augmentation dots (0, 1, or 2). The posterior is dotsVector. Completing the dots step
triggers one call to the evaluation function; if the partial solution is already complete or
fatally erroneous the node is sealed.
This factorisation mirrors the model output structure and keeps the branching factor small at each
decision:O(n)for Pass, 9 for Division, 3 for Dots.
