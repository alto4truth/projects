D Paraff Language and Generation Constraints
Paraffis a domain-specific language (DSL) for symbolic music, designed to be simultaneously
(i) losslessly convertible from/to LilyPond, (ii) unambiguously parseable by a formal grammar,
and (iii) friendly to autoregressive language models through a compact, space-separated token
vocabulary of fewer than 256 tokens. In this paper, Paraff serves as the intermediate representation
for the topology-data generation pipeline described in Section 4.4.2: scores are generated by an
autoregressive model trained on Paraff sequences, then rendered and converted to event-topology
training samples for the BeadSolver. In this conversion, geometric coordinates are taken from
engraving rather than synthesized: Paraff is translated to LilyPond, engraved to SVG, and then Lotus
parses the SVG to recover token geometry (includingx positions) in staff-space units. Layout diversity
is introduced by randomizing engraving conditions (e.g., staff size, page dimensions, raggedness, and
compact layout presets), which changes the resulting spacing while keeping it physically plausible.
Token Vocabulary and Groups
All Paraff tokens are pure alphanumeric strings. Tokens are partitioned intogroups(analogous to
parts of speech), each covering a semantically coherent class of notation elements. See Table 15 for
the principal groups.
