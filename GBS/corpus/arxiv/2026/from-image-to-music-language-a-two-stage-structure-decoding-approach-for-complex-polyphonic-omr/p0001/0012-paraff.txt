A.2 BeadPicker Architecture and Training
BeadPicker is a transformer encoder that reads the full candidate sequence in a single forward pass
and produces predictions for all output heads jointly. See Table 12 for architecture hyperparameters
and Table 13 for training configuration.

The model has ten output heads: successor (next-node selection), tick (absolute tick position),
division (9 categories), dots (3 categories), beam (4 categories), stemDirection (3 categories),
grace, timeWarped, fullMeasure, and fake (binary). The successor head carries the largest
loss weight, since it governs chain structure and is the primary search objective. All other heads use
individually tuned loss weights that down-weight uncertain or rare attributes.

Single-pass BeadSolver uses f= 40 . BeadSolver-Multipass runs three passes with escalating factors
(f= 10 , 40, 80) and returns the best solution found across all passes. The estimateMeasure
pre-pass (version 3) is not a separate model: it uses the same BeadPicker and performs one glimpse
forward pass per cluster under a minimal prefix (only BOS fixed) to populate per-element predispositions, including fullMeasure and tick/duration-related fields, and to re-estimate measure duration
when the time signature is doubtful. This prevents whole-measure rests from being drawn into voice
assignment and substantially reduces irregularTick errors, at the cost of a minor increase in
average tick-twist (see Table 10).
