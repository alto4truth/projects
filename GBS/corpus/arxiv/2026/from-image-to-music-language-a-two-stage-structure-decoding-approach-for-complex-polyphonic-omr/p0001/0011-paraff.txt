Appendices
A Implementation Notes
This appendix collects the engineering details that support reproducibility of the BeadSolver system:
the exact input representation fed to the model, the architecture and training hyperparameters, the
search budget formulas, and the interface used to feed annotated corrections back into the training
pipeline.
A.1 Input Representation
Element types.Each measure is represented as a flat sequence of elements. Five element types are
used: PAD (padding, index 0), BOS (beginning of sequence, index 1), EOS (end of sequence, index 2),
CHORD (index 3), and REST (index 4). In a decoded sequence, the model predicts a successor chain
that begins atBOSand ends atEOS; chords and rests form the body of the structure.
Geometric fields.Each element carries five geometric scalars in staff-space units (one unit = one
staff space): x (horizontal position), pivotX (horizontal anchor, used when the visual centre of mass
is not centred on the event position, e.g. for chords with mixed stem directions), y1 and y2 (upper
and lower staff-vertical extent), and staff (integer staff index within the system, zero-based). During
training, mild data augmentation is applied: x and pivotX receive Perlin noise, y1 and y2 receive
Gaussian noise (σ= 0.12 ), and the whole x-axis is optionally rescaled by a log-normal factor to
simulate horizontal spacing variation.
Local attribute feature vector.In addition to geometry, each element receives a 16-dimensional
real-valued feature vector that encodes soft evidence about local rhythmic and notational attributes,
as estimated by the visual models. These arenothard decisions: the values are log-likelihood-style
scores that convey prior knowledge without committing the model to a specific interpretation. See
Table 11 for the 16 components.
