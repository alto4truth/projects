Question:
Which of the following statements accurately describes the changes observed between the two satellite images of this landscape?
A. A new paved road has been constructed, replacing the dirt path on the left side of the image.
B. A significant portion of the forested area on the right has been cleared, showing new bare ground.
C. Several new small structures or buildings have appeared in the central open area.
D. There are no significant visible changes between the two images.
The correct answer: D
Question: Which of the following statements best describes the transformation observed between the first and second satellite images?
A. Several new structures have been constructed in the open areas on the left side of the image.
B. A large portion of the tree line running vertically along the right side has been cleared.
C. Significant new roads and paths have been established across the open ground.
D. There are no significant visible changes in the land cover, structures, or overall layout of the area.
The correct answer: D.
Question: What is the most accurate observation when comparing these two satellite images of the same area?
A. A new stream or water body has appeared in the central part of the image.
B. Several large trees in the upper left quadrant have been removed.
C. A significant portion of the open ground has been converted into an agricultural field.
D. There are no visible changes between the two images.
The correct answer: D.
