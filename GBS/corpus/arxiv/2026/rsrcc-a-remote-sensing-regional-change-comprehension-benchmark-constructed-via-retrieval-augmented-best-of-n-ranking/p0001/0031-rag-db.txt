A Group-Restricted Retrieval and Boundary Preservation
We formalize the intuition that conditioning on retrieved examples drawn from the model’sown
knowledge groups reduces bias by avoiding out-of-distribution (OOD) exemplars. Intuitively, the
encoder partitions the data manifold into representation groups (clusters) that share semantics and
appearance. Retrieval constrained to the query’s group preserves local decision geometry, whereas
conditioning on examples from other groups can shift the effective decision boundary.
