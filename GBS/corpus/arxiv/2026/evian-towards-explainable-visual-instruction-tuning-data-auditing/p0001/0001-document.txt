Abstract
The efficacy of Large Vision-Language Models
(LVLMs) is critically dependent on the quality of their training data, requiring a precise
balance between visual fidelity and instructionfollowing capability. Existing datasets, however, are plagued by inconsistent quality, and
current data filtering methods rely on coarsegrained scores that lack the granularity to identify nuanced semantic flaws like logical fallacies or factual errors. This creates a fundamental bottleneck in developing more reliable models. To address this, we make three core contributions. First, we construct a large-scale, 300Ksample benchmark by systematically injecting
diverse, subtle defects to provide a challenging
testbed for data auditing. Second, we introduce a novel “Decomposition-then-Evaluation”
paradigm that breaks model responses into constituent cognitive components: visual description, subjective inference, and factual claim,
