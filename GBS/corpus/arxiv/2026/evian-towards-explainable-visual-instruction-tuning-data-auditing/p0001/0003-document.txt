Existing datasets and filtering methods fall short
of this requirement. Large-scale data synthesis
(e.g., LLaV A-Instruct-150K) improves instruction
following but often introduces noise (Liu et al.,
2024c; Tang et al., 2024), while similarity-based
filtering methods (e.g., CLIP score) promote visual
grounding but lack the granularity to detect subtle
semantic flaws (Wang et al., 2024a). As a result,
current LVLMs frequently suffer from fine-grained
errors, including object hallucination, attribute misattribution, factual inconsistency, and flawed reasoning (Liu et al., 2024a; Bai et al., 2024; Chen
