4 Benchmarking Data Quality via
Controlled Defect Injection
To quantitatively validate a data auditing pipeline’s
ability to detect fine-grained flaws in logical coherence, factual accuracy, and image-text consistency,
a tailored benchmark with systematically injected
defects is essential, as existing datasets lack the
controlled errors needed for such a targeted evaluation. To ensure consistency with prior work, we
adopt the SCALE methodology (Xu et al., 2025b)
as the starting point for benchmark construction.
From its source pool of 500,000 multimodal samples across eight datasets (Table 1), we derive two
complementary components: (i) a 50,000-sample
“gold standard” set purified by SCALE, and (ii) a
250,000-sample “challenge” set obtained via random down-sampling followed by our defect injection pipeline. Together, these components yield a
