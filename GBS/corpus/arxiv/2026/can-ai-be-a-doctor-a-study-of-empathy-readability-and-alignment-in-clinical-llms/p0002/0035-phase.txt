Takeaway 3☞Empathy-enhanced prompting (Empathy Prompt) systematically
improves readability and modulates affective tone across both datasets. It reduces
extreme negativity and increases affiliative cues — particularlycaring— while lowering linguistic complexity relative to baseline generations. Readability gains are
especially pronounced for larger architectures such asGPT5, whereas sentiment shifts
are more dataset-dependent: polarity correction is consistent in institutional texts
(MedQuAD) but more variable in conversational dialogue (iCliniqQAs). Overall,
Empathy Prompt acts as a controllable alignment mechanism, steering emotional tone
toward clinical norms while improving accessibility without compromising semantic
fidelity.
4.6 RQ4 – Human–AI Collaboration
This research question evaluates LLMs not only as content generators, but also as editors capable of revising expert-authored medical texts to improve clarity and emotional
resonance.
Hypothesis 4LetR LLM Rephrase denote the physician-authored response rewritten by an
LLM using the Rephrase prompt, andR Phys the original physician response. We hypothesize
that collaborative rewriting will produce responses that are (i)more readableand (ii)more
affectively supportivethan the original physician-authored text, i.e.,
(i)E[Read(R LLM Rephrase)]<E[Read(R Phys)]and
(ii)E[E(R LLM Rephrase)]>E[E(R Phys)].
Rewriting systematically shifts emotional polarity toward more supportive and less
confrontational phrasing across both datasets.
