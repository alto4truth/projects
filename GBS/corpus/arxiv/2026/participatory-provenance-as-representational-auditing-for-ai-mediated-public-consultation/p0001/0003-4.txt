4.7 Cross-topic and multi-model validation
Participants who submitted responses to both consultation topics were identified by internal respondent ID (n= 2,392). Cross-topic consistency of coverage scores was quantified by Spearman
rank correlationρ s; cross-topic consistency of exclusion status (binary) by theϕcoefficient of the
2×2contingency table.
Multi-model robustness was assessed by repeating the full coverage analysis with both robustness
embedding models. For each alternative model, Spearman rank correlation of individual coverage
scores against primary-model scores, and the exclusion agreement rate (fraction of participants
assigned the same exclusion status), were computed. Parameter sensitivity was assessed across
three dimensions: PCA dimensionality (D∈ {20,50,100}), exclusion threshold multiplier (α∈
{0.5,1.0,1.5,2.0}standard deviations below the random mean), and cluster count (k ∗ ±2). For

4.8 Statistical analysis and computational environment
All analyses were conducted in Python 3.11. Core scientific computing used NumPy 1.26, SciPy
1.12 and pandas 2.2. Clustering and causal inference used scikit-learn 1.4 [Pedregosa et al., 2011]
and EconML 0.15. Optimal transport used POT 0.9.3 [Flamary et al., 2021]. LLM inference used
the OpenAI Python SDK 1.17 (GPT-4o-mini,text-embedding-3-large). Local embeddings used
sentence-transformers2.7 [Reimers and Gurevych, 2019]. All bootstrap (B= 2,000) and permutation (N= 10,000) tests used a fixed random seed (numpy.random.seed(42)) for reproducibility.
Statistical significance was assessed atα= 0.05; where multiple comparisons were performed (OLS
interaction terms), Bonferroni correction was applied and full interaction results are reported in
Supplementary Tables 10 and 11.
