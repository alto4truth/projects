An additional pattern in Fig. 5 is the comparatively
narrow SBI posterior forλrelative to MCMC. This
behavior is consistent with an amortization-induced
shrinkage effect in weakly identifiable settings: when
the observation carries limited information about jumparrival intensity, SNPE tends to regularize toward dominant simulator-supported regions learned during training, yielding sharper posteriors. In contrast, MCMC explores a flatter likelihood landscape more explicitly and
therefore retains wider uncertainty forλ[55, 56]. The
key point is that this difference is primarily in posterior
width, not in failure-mode decision; both methods still
produce consistent classification outcomes.
