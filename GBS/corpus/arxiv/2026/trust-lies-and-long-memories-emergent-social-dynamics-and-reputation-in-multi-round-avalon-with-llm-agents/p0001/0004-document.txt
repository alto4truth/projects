Our contributions are as follows:
• We show that LLM agents develop stable, roleconditional reputations when given cross-game memory:
the same player is described as “subtle” when evil but
“straightforward” when good. Agents actively reference
previous games and their learnings when making decisions.
• We find that high-reputation players receive 45.6% more
team inclusions, showing that reputation has downstream strategic consequences for coalition formation.
• We find that higher reasoning enables more sophisticated deception: evil players more frequently pass early
missions to build trust before sabotaging, a strategy
known as “sleeper agent” among human players (75%
at medium/high reasoning vs 36% at low reasoning).
