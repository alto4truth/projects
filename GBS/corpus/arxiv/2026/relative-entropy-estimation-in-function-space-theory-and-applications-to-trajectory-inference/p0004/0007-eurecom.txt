SDE-based models relax determinism and solve a stochastic control problem that steers a Brownian
prior with minimal control effort, typically optimizing only the drift while treating the marginal flow
as fixed or estimated from data. SB-based models are less prescriptive: given a reference diffusion
(e.g., Brownian or Ornstein-Uhlenbeck), they infer a path measure whose snapshot marginals match
the data while remaining close to the reference, equivalently solving a stochastic optimal control
problem (Chen et al., 2021). Yet, computational constraints often push SB methods away from native
path-space representations, relying on discretization and finite-dimensional parameterizations that
only approximately preserve path-space structure.
