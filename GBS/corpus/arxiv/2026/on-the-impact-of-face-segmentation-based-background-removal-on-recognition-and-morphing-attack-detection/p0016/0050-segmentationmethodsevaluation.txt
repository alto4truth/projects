When evaluating how background removal techniques
affect downstream tasks such as FR and MAD, it is important
to keep in mind that the verified performance changes might
derive from inappropriate background removal. While it is
impossible to ensure perfect segmentation, the quality of
the employed segmentation techniques should be enforced
in advance to ensure that verified performance fluctuations
can be mapped to the act of background removal, and not
to the specific technique being employed. In this section, we
describe the experimental setup and results of this evaluation.
